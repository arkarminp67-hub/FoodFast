## 1. Target User Typologies & Behavioral Personas

### Persona A: The Time-Constrained Student
*   **Context:** Back-to-back lectures with strict 15-to-30-minute transitional break windows.
*   **Incidents Observed:** Students rushing from lecture buildings to the cafeteria, scanning the long queues at popular noodle and rice stalls, and leaving empty-handed to avoid being late to their next lab session.
*   **Pain Points:** Spending 75% of their total break duration standing in a static line; high mental anxiety concerning lecture tardiness.

### Persona B: The Busy Faculty Member
*   **Context:** Lecturers and administrative staff with tightly scheduled grading periods, student consultations, or faculty meetings.
*   **Incidents Observed:** Professors standing in line for long periods, checking their watches, and expressing frustration when manual paper ticketing systems mix up their order queue.
*   **Pain Points:** Complete lack of ordering visibility; unable to gauge if food preparation will take 5 minutes or 20 minutes.

---

## 2. Current Market Alternatives & Workarounds
To cope with the operational bottlenecks of the current cafeteria environment, target users rely on three inefficient alternatives:
1.  **Skipping Meals Entirely:** Buying snacks from vending machines or ignoring hunger cues, which directly impacts energy and classroom concentration.
2.  **Off-Peak Dining Schedules:** Eating lunch early at 10:45 AM or waiting until 2:30 PM to avoid crowds, throwing off standard schedules.
3.  **The "Line Scout" System:** Sending a single friend ahead to order for an entire group, creating friction for other customers in line.

---

## 3. Downstream Consequences
*   **For Users:** Decreased classroom engagement, nutritional neglect, and chronic punctuality issues with afternoon classes.
*   **For Campus Food Vendors:** Severe operational stress during peak hours, human error from processing paper tickets, lost revenue from customers who walk away, and an inability to forecast ingredient demand accurately.
